QUICK REFERENCE GUIDE - STOCK INVESTMENT CHECKLIST
================================================================================

BEFORE BUYING ANY STOCK - THE 5-MINUTE CHECK:
==============================================

1. FINANCIALS (30 seconds):
   [ ] PE < 15 (not overvalued)
   [ ] Debt/Equity < 1 (low debt)
   [ ] ROE > 15% (profitable)

2. PRICE ACTION (30 seconds):
   [ ] Down 30%+ from 52-week high (discounted)
   [ ] Price > Rs. 20 (avoid penny stocks)
   [ ] Good trading volume (can sell easily)

3. PROMOTER CHECK (1 minute):
   [ ] Holding > 40% (skin in game)
   [ ] Pledging = 0% (no desperation)
   [ ] No recent selling by insiders

4. RECENT RESULTS (2 minutes):
   [ ] Read last quarter summary
   [ ] Check profit growth trend
   [ ] Any red flags in commentary

5. NEWS CHECK (1 minute):
   [ ] Google: "[Stock name] news"
   [ ] Any legal issues? Scams? Frauds?
   [ ] Recent management changes?


POSITION SIZING FORMULA:
========================

For Rs. 20,000 portfolio:
- Min per stock: Rs. 2,000 (10%)
- Max per stock: Rs. 5,000 (25%)
- Ideal: 4-5 stocks of Rs. 3,000-5,000 each

Never exceed 25% in one stock!


STOP-LOSS CALCULATION:
======================

Long-term stocks:
Stop-Loss = Buy Price × 0.85 (15% below)
Example: Bought at Rs. 100 → Stop-Loss = Rs. 85

Swing trades:
Stop-Loss = Buy Price × 0.92 (8% below)
Example: Bought at Rs. 100 → Stop-Loss = Rs. 92


TARGET CALCULATION:
===================

Conservative Target:
Target = Buy Price × 1.20 (20% profit)

Aggressive Target:
Target = Buy Price × 1.40 (40% profit)

Always maintain 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio!


PORTFOLIO REBALANCING TRIGGERS:
================================

Rebalance when:
1. Any stock > 40% of portfolio
2. Quarterly review shows weak fundamentals
3. Stock hits target price
4. Better opportunity available
5. Sector allocation > 50%


SECTOR ALLOCATION LIMITS:
=========================

For Rs. 20,000 portfolio:

Safe allocation:
- Banking: Max 25%
- IT: Max 20%
- Pharma: Max 20%
- Auto: Max 15%
- Chemicals: Max 15%
- Others: Max 5% each

Never put >40% in one sector!


SWING TRADING RULES:
====================

Entry Conditions (ALL must be YES):
[ ] Stock in overall uptrend
[ ] Currently pulling back (RSI 30-45)
[ ] Good volume (avg > 100,000 shares/day)
[ ] Clear support level visible
[ ] No major event/results in next 2 weeks

Exit Conditions (ANY ONE is YES):
[ ] 15-20% profit achieved
[ ] 7-10% loss (stop-loss hit)
[ ] 3 months passed (re-evaluate)
[ ] Fundamentals deteriorated
[ ] Better opportunity found

Max holding: 2 swing trades at a time
Max per trade: Rs. 5,000


WHEN TO SELL - THE EXIT CHECKLIST:
===================================

SELL IMMEDIATELY if:
1. Stop-loss triggered (no emotions!)
2. Promoter pledging > 20%
3. Major fraud/scam news
4. Auditor resignation
5. Debt increased sharply (>50% in one quarter)

CONSIDER SELLING if:
1. Target price achieved
2. 2 consecutive quarters of profit decline
3. Stock becomes >40% of portfolio
4. Sector faces long-term headwinds
5. Found better opportunity

NEVER SELL because:
1. Stock fell 5-10% (normal volatility)
2. Market panic (unless fundamentals changed)
3. Friend/expert said so (do your research)
4. Boredom (give time to thesis)


AVERAGING DOWN RULES:
=====================

Only average down if:
[ ] Fundamentals still strong
[ ] Quarterly results still good
[ ] Fall due to market, not company-specific
[ ] Have conviction for 2+ years
[ ] Not already >25% of portfolio

How much to average:
- 1st average: 50% of original position
- 2nd average: 25% of original position
- Never average more than twice

Example:
Bought 20 shares @ Rs. 100 = Rs. 2,000
Stock falls to Rs. 80
1st average: 10 shares @ Rs. 80 = Rs. 800
New average: Rs. 93.33

RED FLAGS - NEVER BUY IF:
=========================

1. Promoter pledging > 20%
2. Declining promoter holding (selling stake)
3. Frequent auditor changes
4. Related-party transactions > 20% of revenue
5. Negative operating cash flow for 2+ quarters
6. Debt/Equity > 3 (except banks)
7. Frequent bonus/split announcements (red flag)
8. Stock in T2T segment (trade-to-trade)
9. Price < Rs. 10 (penny stock)
10. No trading volume (< 10,000 shares/day)


DAILY ROUTINE (5 MINUTES):
==========================

Morning (before market opens):
1. Check SGX Nifty (indicates market direction)
2. Scan financial news headlines
3. Check any company-specific news for your holdings

During Market Hours:
1. Place limit orders (never market orders)
2. Don't panic on 2-3% moves
3. Only check prices 2-3 times (avoid overtrading)

Evening (after market close):
1. Update portfolio tracker if any transactions
2. Set price alerts for next day
3. Check if any stop-loss/target hit


WEEKLY ROUTINE (30 MINUTES):
============================

Every Sunday:
1. Update current prices in tracker (5 min)
2. Run screener query for new ideas (10 min)
3. Read news for portfolio stocks (10 min)
4. Plan next week's actions (5 min)


MONTHLY ROUTINE (2 HOURS):
==========================

Last Sunday of month:
1. Calculate monthly return (10 min)
2. Review each holding individually (60 min)
3. Check quarterly results if released (30 min)
4. Rebalance if needed (20 min)


USEFUL WEBSITES:
================

Screening: screener.in

News: moneycontrol.com, economictimes.com
Results: screener.in (Quarterly Results section)
Annual Reports: bseindia.com or company website


BROKER TIPS:
============

1. Use LIMIT orders, not MARKET orders
2. Set GTD (Good Till Day) orders
3. Enable price alerts for your stocks
4. Check brokerage charges (should be <1%)
5. Use bracket orders for swing trades (auto stop-loss)


COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID:
=========================

1. Buying without research (FOMO)
2. Not setting stop-loss
3. Averaging down on falling knife
4. Selling winners, keeping losers
5. Checking prices every 5 minutes
6. Trading on tips/rumors
7. Investing borrowed money
8. Putting all money at once
9. Ignoring quarterly results
10. Emotional attachment to stocks


PORTFOLIO HEALTH CHECK:
=======================

Monthly check - Your portfolio should have:

[ ] 4-6 stocks (diversification)
[ ] No stock > 30% allocation
[ ] No sector > 40% allocation
[ ] 10-15% cash reserve
[ ] All stocks have stop-loss set
[ ] All stocks fundamentally sound
[ ] At least 3 different sectors
[ ] Mix of large/mid caps


MOTIVATION REMINDERS:
=====================

1. "Time in market > Timing the market"
2. Patience is your biggest weapon
3. Small consistent gains > home runs
4. 20% annual return = excellent (don't be greedy)
5. Learn from mistakes, keep notes
6. Focus on process, not just profits
7. Sleep-well-at-night portfolio is best
8. You don't need to trade every day
9. Cash is also a position
10. Compound interest is magic (give it time)
