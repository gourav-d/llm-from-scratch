❌ Technical jargon first

### After (New Style):
```
What Is Loss?

Loss = A report card that measures how wrong the model is.

Think of it like archery:
- Hit bullseye → Loss = 0 (perfect!)
- Miss by 1 foot → Loss = 1 (pretty good)
- Miss by 10 feet → Loss = 10 (terrible!)

For GPT predicting next word:
- Model says "mat" (80% confident), correct answer is "mat"
  → Loss = 0.22 (low = good!)

- Model says "table" (60% confident), correct answer is "mat"
  → Loss = 1.2 (high = bad!)

Technical Formula: L = -log(probability of correct answer)
```
✅ Simple definition first
✅ Real-world analogy
✅ Concrete example with numbers
✅ Technical details last

> correct mistakes, repeat millions of times until they learn!"

**Fine-Tuning:**
> "Fine-tuning is like hiring a college graduate and giving job

> your specific task. 100x faster and cheaper than starting

**Loss Function:**
> "Loss is your model's report card - low score = good predictions,

> "Validation is like a practice exam - tests if the model truly
> understands or just memorized the training data!"
